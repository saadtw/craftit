CRITICAL: production-ack route — scope split
The bid_acceptance prompt repurposes app/api/orders/[id]/production-ack/route.js into a non-blocking production start notification for bid-based orders.
The craftit prompt lists this route as "already implemented — do not re-implement" in the context of group buy partial payment handling.
Resolution: the group buy cron (complete-group-buys) creates orders directly and does not call the production-ack route. The bid_acceptance repurpose of this route is therefore safe. When updating this route, ensure it only acts on orders where status === "confirmed" (bid-based orders) and does not interfere with group buy orders in accepted status.

Group buy MVQ failure — manufacturer decision window (not in either prompt)
The craftit prompt auto-cancels a group buy immediately when the minimum viable quantity is not met after payment capture attempts. This needs a small addition to match the intended product behaviour:
When paidQuantity < groupBuy.minimumViableQuantity after the capture + retry cycle completes:

Do not immediately cancel. Set groupBuy.status = "mvq_review" (add this to the GroupBuy status enum).
Notify the manufacturer: "The group buy fell below minimum quantity. You have 24 hours to decide: fulfil at the discounted price for the X customers who paid, or cancel and refund everyone."
Create a new route POST /api/group-buys/[id]/mvq-decision with body { decision: "fulfil" | "cancel" }:

fulfil: creates individual orders for all participants with status === "paid", sets groupBuy.status = "completed", notifies paid customers their order is created, notifies non-paying customers their spot is dropped.
cancel: refunds all paid participants via Stripe, sets groupBuy.status = "cancelled", notifies everyone.


If no decision is made within 24 hours, a cron (add to complete-group-buys or a separate job) auto-cancels and refunds everyone.
